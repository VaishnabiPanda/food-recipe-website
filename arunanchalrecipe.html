Bamboo Shoot Curry

Ingredients: Bamboo shoots, garlic, ginger, onion, green chili, oil, chicken or pork, turmeric, salt, water, rice.
Time: 30 minutes.
Method: Boil sliced bamboo shoots to reduce bitterness. Saute garlic, ginger, onion, and green chili in oil. Add chicken or pork if desired and cook until browned. Add bamboo shoots, turmeric, salt, and water, then simmer until tender. Serve with steamed rice.

Thukpa

Ingredients: Noodles, garlic, ginger, onion, green chili, carrots, cabbage, greens, stock, salt, pepper, spring onion, coriander.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Boil noodles and keep aside. Saute garlic, ginger, onion, green chili, carrots, cabbage, and greens. Add stock, salt, and pepper, then simmer. Add cooked noodles and garnish with spring onion and coriander.

Momos

Ingredients: Flour, water, salt, minced meat or vegetables, onion, ginger, garlic, chili, coriander, tomato chutney.
Time: 30 minutes.
Method: Make dough with flour, water, and salt. Prepare filling with minced meat or vegetables, onion, ginger, garlic, chili, coriander, and salt. Fill circles of dough, pleat, seal, and steam for 15 to 20 minutes. Serve with spicy tomato chutney.

Lukter

Ingredients: Smoked beef or pork, dry red chilies, salt, onion, fermented soybean.
Time: 6 to 8 hours plus 30 minutes.
Method: Use smoked beef or pork and slice it thinly. Roast dry red chilies and crush them coarsely. Mix the smoked meat with chili flakes and salt. Add chopped onion or fermented soybean if desired. Serve as a spicy side with rice.

Pehak

Ingredients: Soybeans, roasted chilies, garlic, salt, mustard oil.
Time: 6 to 8 hours plus 30 minutes.
Method: Boil soybeans until soft and ferment them for a few days. Mash into a coarse paste. Grind with roasted chilies, garlic, and salt. Add mustard oil if desired and serve with rice or meat dishes.

Apong

Ingredients: Rice, traditional fermentation starter, water.
Time: 3 to 5 days.
Method: Cook rice and cool it completely. Mix with a traditional fermentation starter and keep in a clean container for several days. Strain the fermented liquid before serving. It is a traditional festive rice drink.
