Mui Borok

Ingredients: Vegetables or fish, berma, green chili, garlic, salt, rice.
Time: 6 to 8 hours plus 30 minutes.
Method: Cook vegetables or fish with berma, the traditional fermented fish. Add green chili, garlic, and salt. Simmer until flavours blend and vegetables become tender. Serve with steamed rice.

Wahan Mosdeng

Ingredients: Pork, green chili, onion, coriander, ginger, salt, lime juice.
Time: 1 hour.
Method: Roast or boil pork and chop into small pieces. Mix with roasted green chili, onion, coriander, ginger, and salt. Add a little lime juice if desired. Serve as a spicy pork salad.

Gudok

Ingredients: Vegetables, potato, bamboo shoot, beans, berma, garlic, chili, salt.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Boil vegetables, potato, bamboo shoot, and beans with berma, garlic, chili, and salt. Mash lightly after cooking. Serve thick and rustic with rice.

Chakhwi

Ingredients: Papaya, bamboo shoot, jackfruit seeds, pork or fish, alkali water, ginger, chili, salt.
Time: 1 hour.
Method: Cook vegetables such as papaya, bamboo shoot, and jackfruit seeds with pork or fish. Add alkali water if available, ginger, chili, and salt. Simmer until soft and soupy.

Mosdeng Serma

Ingredients: Tomatoes, chilies, onion, coriander, salt, berma.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Roast tomatoes and chilies. Mash with onion, coriander, salt, and berma. Mix into a smoky chutney. Serve with rice and boiled vegetables.

Bhangui

Ingredients: Rice, lairu leaves or banana leaves, ghee, ginger.
Time: 30 minutes.
Method: Wash rice and wrap it in lairu leaves or banana leaves with a little ghee and ginger. Tie and boil or steam until cooked. Serve during festivals with meat or chutney.
