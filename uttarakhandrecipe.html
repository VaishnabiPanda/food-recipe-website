Kafuli

Ingredients: Spinach, fenugreek leaves, ghee, garlic, ginger, green chili, cumin, asafoetida, rice flour, rice.
Time: 30 minutes.
Method: Blanch spinach and fenugreek leaves, then grind into paste. Cook in ghee with garlic, ginger, green chili, cumin, and asafoetida. Add rice flour slurry to thicken and simmer. Serve with steamed rice.

Chainsoo

Ingredients: Black gram dal, ghee, cumin, garlic, coriander powder, turmeric, chili powder, water.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Dry-roast black gram dal until aromatic, then grind coarsely. Cook in ghee with cumin, garlic, coriander powder, turmeric, chili powder, and water. Simmer until thick and earthy. Serve with rice.

Aloo Ke Gutke

Ingredients: Potatoes, mustard oil, jakhiya or cumin, dry red chili, turmeric, coriander powder, chili powder, salt.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Boil potatoes and cube them. Cook mustard oil with jakhiya or cumin, dry red chili, turmeric, coriander powder, chili powder, and salt. Toss potatoes until coated and slightly crisp. Serve with puri or dal.

Jhangora Ki Kheer

Ingredients: Barnyard millet, milk, sugar, cardamom, nuts.
Time: 40 minutes.
Method: Wash barnyard millet and cook in milk until soft. Add sugar, cardamom, and nuts. Simmer until creamy. Serve warm or chilled during festivals.

Phaanu

Ingredients: Mixed lentils, garlic, ginger, cumin, turmeric, coriander powder, water, ghee.
Time: Overnight soaking plus 30 minutes.
Method: Soak mixed lentils overnight and grind coarsely. Cook with garlic, ginger, cumin, turmeric, coriander powder, and water until thick. Temper with ghee and serve with rice.

Singori

Ingredients: Khoya, sugar, cardamom, coconut, malu leaves.
Time: 30 minutes.
Method: Cook khoya with sugar, cardamom, and grated coconut until thick. Cool slightly and wrap small portions in malu leaves or serve as cones. Let set before eating.
